Process We Follow

We tailor the web development process to your unique needs and requirements. No two processes are identical because we want your website or web application to be as personalized and specific to your business requirements as possible. That said, these are the general steps we follow.

Client Discovery and Requirement Gathering:

The process begins with a thorough understanding of the client's goals, target audience, and specific requirements. This stage involves client meetings, surveys, and research to gather all necessary information.

Project Planning:

Once requirements are gathered, a project plan is created. This plan outlines the project scope, timeline, budget, and resources required. It serves as a roadmap for the entire development process.

Design and Wireframing:

The design phase involves creating wireframes and mockups to visualize the website's layout, structure, and user interface. Client feedback is crucial at this stage to ensure the design aligns with their vision.

Front-End Development:

Front-end development involves converting the approved designs into actual web pages. HTML, CSS, and JavaScript are used to build the website's user interface, ensuring it's responsive and accessible across devices.

Back-End Development:

Back-end development focuses on building the server-side functionality of the website. This includes database design and handles data storage, user authentication, and server-side logic.

Database Development:

A database is designed and implemented to store and manage website data efficiently. Common database systems include MySQL

Testing and Quality Assurance:

This includes functional testing, usability testing, performance testing, and security testing.

Client Review and Feedback:

The client is given the opportunity to review the website at various stages of development and provide feedback for revisions and improvements.

Deployment:

Once the website is fully tested and approved, it is deployed to a live server, making it accessible to the public.

Regular Updates and Enhancements:

Websites require ongoing updates, enhancements, and content management to stay current and effective.

Reporting and Communication:

Regular reporting and communication with the client ensure that the website continues to meet their objectives and that any necessary adjustments are made.
